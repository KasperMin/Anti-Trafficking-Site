What are Major Gifts?

Major gifts are basically big donations. But the aspect that differentiate major gifts from small donations, besides overtly the size, are the process by which they are obtained. It is also characterized by the significance it has to the donor as well as your NGO, which makes it a matter of subjectivity. Here are two different definitions that helps shed a light on major gifts:
  • "We’ve (red. fundraising professionals) been using this term (red. major gifts) for a very long time, and while I don’t know where it actually came from, I do know it doesn’t necessarily refer to major money. Major gifts can mean major transformation - they can lead to major change." (Parrish & Walters, 2016)
  • " Major gifts are those gifts that can fund important projects and programs, demonstrate the donor’s belief in the organization’s mission and have a transformational impact on those priority programs, projects and the entire organization. It is not the number of zeros but the impact. (AFP, 2015)"
The pivotal element in defining major gifts are not the size of the gift but the impact it has on your NGO. If your NGO is small, then a gift worth $3000 might be considered a major gift, while this for most large organizations probably would be considered a small donation.
Considering this, it makes sense that process is different from that of a small donation, since a donor need to trust your NGO and deeply care about its cause before donation a big chunk of her or his hard earned money. That's why major a major gift often is customized for the donor and target for a specific project or program, though that is not always the case (Green, 2015).
