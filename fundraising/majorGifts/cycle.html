The Major Gift Cycle

The Major Gift Cycle is a term that describes the process and time frame that your NGO goes through during a major gifts program. It consists of 4 phases or sub-processes which are: Identification, Cultivation, Solicitation and Stewardship.

Step 1: Identification

This is the process by which your NGO identify the individuals who are likely, and capable, of giving a big gift. The assessment is based on three parameters: their ability to give, their interest in the cause, and their connection to your organization (Institute of Fundraising, 2015). These individuals are referred to as prospects.

Step 2: Cultivation

This is the grooming process where your NGO builds a relationship with the prospect. Donors want to trust and like individuals at the organization, hence the expression "people give to people" (Eisenstein, A2). This includes a plan for how and when to meet with the individuals on your list and get a deeper understanding of who they are.

Step 3: Solicitation

This is asking the donor to make a gift, also referred to as the ask. This step entails calling the prospect to set up the meeting and also preparing how to conduct the meeting since there are some important details to be aware of.

Step 4: Stewardship

This is the last part of the process. This is where your NGO show your gratitude to the donor. But this step needs to be thought of long before the ask. Here it is also crucial to create a plan on who, when and how the follow up will be executed.
